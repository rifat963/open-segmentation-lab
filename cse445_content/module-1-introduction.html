Module 1

Introduction to Computer Vision

Understand what computer vision is, how it differs from image processing, and how complete systems transform visual data into decisions.

Lecture 1: Introduction to Computer Vision

Define computer vision, distinguish it from digital image processing, and connect the field to autonomous driving, medical imaging, robotics, agriculture, and surveillance.

Lecture 2: Vision Tasks and System Pipeline

Compare classification, detection, segmentation, tracking, and 3D vision. Trace the pipeline from image input to preprocessing, feature extraction, inference, and output.

Infographic

A compact view of the end-to-end vision pipeline.

1

Capture

Camera or sensor records pixels under real lighting, viewpoint, and motion constraints.

2

Prepare

Resize, normalize, denoise, crop, or enhance the image so later stages receive stable input.

3

Infer

Classical features or learned models estimate categories, boxes, masks, tracks, or geometry.

4

Act

The system reports a diagnosis, avoids an obstacle, counts objects, or sends a human-readable result.

Scenario Driven Exploration

Autonomous shuttle at night

List which tasks are required to detect pedestrians, localize lane boundaries, track nearby vehicles, and estimate free space. Decide where preprocessing is most important.

Hospital triage assistant

Separate the parts of the pipeline that enhance images from the parts that interpret disease patterns. Explain why this distinction matters for accountability.
