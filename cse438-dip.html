CSE438 Digital Image Processing

An undergraduate course covering the fundamentals of image processing — from spatial and frequency domain techniques to morphological operations and basic segmentation.

Course Overview

CSE438 introduces students to the theory and practice of digital image processing. The course builds from pixel-level operations to system-level pipelines used in medical imaging, remote sensing, and computer vision pre-processing.

Course Code

CSE438 — Digital Image Processing
Department of Computer Science and Engineering
East West University, Dhaka

Prerequisites

Linear algebra, probability, introductory programming (Python or MATLAB). Familiarity with NumPy is beneficial.

Topics Covered

Module 1 — Image Fundamentals

  • Digital image representation and sampling
  • Pixel relationships, connectivity, and regions
  • Intensity transformations and histogram operations

Module 2 — Spatial Filtering

  • Linear convolution and correlation
  • Smoothing filters: mean, Gaussian
  • Sharpening filters: Laplacian, unsharp masking

Module 3 — Frequency Domain

  • Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) and its properties
  • Low-pass, high-pass, and band-pass filtering
  • Butterworth and Gaussian frequency filters

Module 4 — Morphological Processing

  • Erosion, dilation, opening, and closing
  • Hit-or-miss transform and boundary extraction
  • Connected components and region labeling

Module 5 — Image Segmentation

  • Edge detection: Sobel, Canny
  • Thresholding: Otsu's method, adaptive thresholding
  • Region growing and watershed

Module 6 — Image Restoration

  • Noise models: Gaussian, salt-and-pepper, Poisson
  • Mean and order-statistic filters
  • Wiener filter and inverse filtering concepts

Recommended Resources

Primary Textbook

Gonzalez, R. C., & Woods, R. E. (2018). Digital Image Processing (4th ed.). Pearson. Chapters 2–10 map directly to the course modules.

Software Tools

Python with OpenCV and scikit-image for lab assignments. MATLAB Image Processing Toolbox is accepted as an alternative.
