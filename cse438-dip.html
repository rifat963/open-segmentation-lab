Course Objective

This course introduces fundamental concepts and techniques of digital image processing and their applications in real-life problems. It covers image formation, human visual perception, two-dimensional data representation, spatial and frequency domain processing, filtering, enhancement, Fourier transform techniques, color image processing, segmentation, and morphology.

Course Outcomes

  • CO1: Explain image basics, sampling, quantization, color, transformations, and histograms.
  • CO2: Apply spatial and frequency-domain filtering methods.
  • CO3: Model restoration, segmentation, morphology, and shape recognition.
  • CO4: Demonstrate labs, communicate concepts, and write application reports.

Laboratory dashboard

The lab component runs in parallel with the lectures and asks students to implement, analyze, and report.

Labs 1-6: Representation, Enhancement and Filtering

  • Python tools, image I/O, color channels, histograms
  • Sampling, quantization, pixel relationships, operations
  • Point operations and gray-level transformations
  • Histogram equalization and matching
  • Smoothing filters with convolution and order-statistics
  • Sharpening, unsharp masking, and high boost filtering

Labs 7-12: Edges, Frequency, Restoration and Regions

  • Derivative filters and edge detection
  • DFT and frequency domain filters
  • DCT, wavelets, compression artifacts, Hough transform
  • Noise modeling and restoration filters
  • Thresholding and region-based segmentation
  • Morphological operations and shape descriptors

Term Project

The course includes a proposal, report, presentation, and project demonstration mapped to CO4. Expected sections include abstract, background, problem statement, objectives, research questions, related work, materials and methods, results and discussion, conclusion, limitations, and future work.

Teaching Materials and Software

Primary text: R. C. Gonzalez and R. E. Woods, Digital Image Processing, Prentice-Hall.

References: A. K. Jain, Fundamentals of Digital Image Processing; T. Acharya and A. K. Ray, Image Processing: Principles and Applications.

Software: Python with NumPy, OpenCV, SciPy, scikit-image, PyWavelets, and Matplotlib.
