Reading list

Segmentation Papers

This reading list is organized for students moving from classical segmentation architectures to transformer models, self-supervised learning, and promptable foundation models.

Foundational Segmentation Papers

2015

Fully Convolutional Networks for Semantic Segmentation

Introduced end-to-end dense prediction by converting classification CNNs into fully convolutional networks for pixel-wise labeling.

Summary: This is the core paper for understanding why segmentation can be trained as a spatial prediction problem rather than patch-by-patch classification. Students should focus on upsampling, skip connections, and mean IoU evaluation.

Read paper

2015

U-Net: Convolutional Networks for Biomedical Image Segmentation

Established an encoder-decoder architecture with skip connections for precise biomedical segmentation from limited annotated data.

Summary: U-Net is useful for learning how high-level context and fine boundary detail are combined. Its skip connections remain a standard design idea in medical, agricultural, and small-data segmentation.

Read paper

2017

Mask R-CNN

Added a mask branch to Faster R-CNN, making instance segmentation a natural extension of object detection.

Summary: The paper is important for understanding proposal-based instance segmentation. Pay attention to RoIAlign, the parallel mask head, and how box detection and mask prediction are evaluated differently.

Read paper

2019

Panoptic Segmentation

Formalized a unified task that combines semantic segmentation for stuff regions with instance segmentation for countable things.

Summary: This paper defines the panoptic task and Panoptic Quality. It is the right starting point for studying things versus stuff and understanding why semantic and instance outputs need reconciliation.

Read paper

Transformer-based Segmentation Papers

2021

SegFormer

Combined hierarchical transformer encoders with a lightweight all-MLP decoder for semantic segmentation.

Summary: SegFormer is a clean example of transformer-based semantic segmentation. It is useful for studying hierarchical features, simple decoders, and robustness across common segmentation benchmarks.

Read paper

2021

Swin Transformer

Introduced shifted-window attention and hierarchical transformer features that transfer well to dense prediction tasks.

Summary: Swin is not only a segmentation paper, but it is a major backbone paper. Students should note how local window attention and shifted windows reduce cost while preserving cross-window information.

Read paper

2022

Mask2Former

Unified semantic, instance, and panoptic segmentation using masked attention and mask classification.

Summary: Mask2Former is central for modern universal segmentation. It reframes segmentation around predicted masks and localized attention, making one architecture family work across multiple segmentation tasks.

Read paper

Self-supervised Segmentation Papers

2020

SimCLR

Popularized a simple contrastive learning framework for visual representation learning.

Summary: SimCLR is a useful entry point for contrastive pretraining. For segmentation students, it explains how augmentations and representation learning can improve encoders before dense labels are available.

Read paper

2021

DINO: Emerging Properties in Self-Supervised Vision Transformers

Showed that self-distilled vision transformers can learn object-centric representations without labels.

Summary: DINO is especially relevant to segmentation because self-supervised attention maps can reveal object-like regions. It helps students connect representation learning with pseudo-mask discovery.

Read paper

2022

Masked Autoencoders Are Scalable Vision Learners

Demonstrated masked image modeling as an efficient pretraining approach for vision transformers.

Summary: MAE trains encoders by reconstructing missing patches. It is important for segmentation because masked pretraining can produce strong visual features before task-specific mask labels are introduced.

Read paper

Segment Anything and Foundation Model Papers

2023

Segment Anything

Introduced a promptable segmentation foundation model and a large-scale mask dataset.

Summary: SAM is important for annotation workflows and promptable segmentation. Students should understand that it predicts masks from prompts but does not automatically assign task-specific semantic labels.

Read paper

2023

Fast Segment Anything

Proposed a faster CNN-based approach for segment-anything style workflows.

Summary: FastSAM is useful for discussing the trade-off between foundation-model flexibility and practical speed. It frames promptable segmentation as all-instance segmentation followed by prompt-guided selection.

Read paper

2024

SAM 2: Segment Anything in Images and Videos

Extended promptable segmentation from images toward video with memory-based object tracking across frames.

Summary: SAM 2 is relevant for students interested in video segmentation and interactive annotation. It shows how promptable image segmentation can be adapted to temporal mask propagation.

Read paper
