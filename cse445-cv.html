Summer 2026 Course Outline

CSE445 Computer Vision

A 4-credit undergraduate course on how machines perceive, interpret, and reason about images and videos through features, geometry, motion, recognition, tracking, and real-world vision systems.

Course Snapshot

Based on the CSE445 Summer 2026 course outline from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, East West University.

Theory plus laboratory, 4 credits total.

Contact5 hrs/week

3 hours theory and 2 hours lab across the semester.

Dr. Mohammad Rifat Ahmmad Rashid, Room 646.

Course Objective

This course introduces fundamental concepts, methods, and system-level perspectives of computer vision. It moves beyond pixel-level image processing into feature-level representation, geometric reasoning, motion analysis, and learning-based visual inference for end-to-end vision systems.

Course Outcomes

  • CO1: Explain fundamental concepts, visual structure, applications, and challenges.
  • CO2: Apply feature-based and geometry-based methods for correspondences and spatial relationships.
  • CO3: Interpret motion, 3D vision, recognition, and learning-based pipelines.
  • CO4: Design, implement, and document complete vision systems.

Open ModuleLab Plan

Laboratory Track

The lab sequence supports CO4 through implementation, result analysis, reporting, and a mini project.

Labs 1-5: Foundations and Geometry

  • Image representation and basic operations
  • Harris and FAST feature detection
  • SIFT and ORB feature extraction and matching
  • Camera calibration with Zhang's method
  • Homography and panorama stitching

Labs 6-10: Depth, Motion, Detection, Tracking

  • Stereo vision and depth estimation
  • Optical flow estimation
  • Object detection with HOG+SVM
  • Object recognition with YOLOv8
  • Multi-object tracking with SORT or DeepSORT

Teaching Materials and Tools

Primary book: Richard Szeliski, Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications, 2nd edition.

References: Hartley and Zisserman, Multiple View Geometry in Computer Vision; Forsyth and Ponce, Computer Vision: A Modern Approach; Bishop, Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning.

Software: Anaconda, Google Colab, OpenCV, Python data tools, and appropriate tools for data wrangling and experiment reporting.
