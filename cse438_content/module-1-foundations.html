Module 1

DIP Foundations and Image Representation

Build the base vocabulary of digital images: pixels, sampling, quantization, neighborhoods, distances, and arithmetic or logical image operations.

Lecture Focus

Mapped to CO1 from the Summer 2026 course outline.

Lectures 1-2: Introduction and Key Stages

Define digital image processing, identify real-life applications, and trace the core stages from acquisition to output.

Lectures 3-5: Sampling, Quantization, Pixels and Distance

Analyze sampling rate, quantization levels, pixel adjacency, 4-connected and 8-connected neighborhoods, and common distance measures.

Lecture 6: Arithmetic and Logical Operations

Apply image addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, masking, blending, and Boolean operations for basic image manipulation.

Scenario Driven Exploration

Low-bandwidth medical upload

A clinic must upload X-ray previews over a slow network. Decide how much downsampling and quantization can be tolerated before diagnostic structures become unreliable.

Industrial mask alignment

A factory camera compares a part image with a reference mask. Explain which pixel operations and distance measures help identify missing regions.
