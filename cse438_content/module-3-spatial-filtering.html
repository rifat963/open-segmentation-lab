Module 3

Spatial Filtering and Derivative Operators

Use masks and kernels directly in the image plane to smooth noise, sharpen detail, and expose edges.

Lecture Focus

Mapped to CO2 from the course outline.

Lectures 12-13: Fundamentals and Smoothing Filters

Neighborhood processing, convolution, correlation, mean filters, weighted average filters, Gaussian filters, median filters, and order-statistics filters.

Lectures 14-15: Sharpening, Unsharp Masking and High Boost

Laplacian sharpening, blurred-image subtraction, high-boost filtering, boost constants, edge sharpness, and noise amplification.

Lecture 16: First-Order and Second-Order Derivative Filters

Sobel, Prewitt, Laplacian, Laplacian of Gaussian, Canny edge detection, and noise sensitivity across operators.

Infographic

Choosing a spatial filter.

N

Noise problem

Use smoothing or order-statistics filters when random variation dominates.

D

Detail problem

Use sharpening or high boost when important boundaries look soft.

E

Edge problem

Use derivative filters when changes in intensity are the target signal.

T

Trade-off

Balance denoising, edge preservation, and artifact risk.

Scenario Driven Exploration

Crack inspection image

A concrete image has fine cracks and sensor noise. Decide where smoothing helps and where it destroys the evidence you need.

Text scan enhancement

A scanned document is blurred. Compare sharpening, high boost, and edge detection for making the text readable without ringing artifacts.
